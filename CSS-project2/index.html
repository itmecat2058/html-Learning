Drivers

What are drivers?

A graphics driver is a program that controls how a computer's graphics card interacts with the rest of the computer.

What do they do?

Graphics drivers translate digital code into images, videos, and pictures that appear on the computer screen.

How do they work?

Graphics drivers act as a communication bridge between the computer's software and hardware. They provide instructions to the graphics card, which is a physical component that renders images on the screen.

Why are they important

Graphics drivers are essential for using a computer's graphics card to play games and view other content. Outdated or faulty graphics drivers can cause issues like laggy gameplay, low-quality graphics, and crashes.
